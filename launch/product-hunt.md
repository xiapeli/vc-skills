# Product Hunt Launch Materials

## Tagline (60 chars max)
Pitch to AI VCs before you pitch for real

## Description
VC Skills turns your AI coding assistant into a venture capital partner. Not a generic chatbot — a specific simulation of how Sequoia, a16z, YC, Benchmark, and 23 other VCs actually evaluate startups.

Drop a .md file into Claude Code, Cursor, or ChatGPT. The AI asks the questions that firm asks. Pushes back where they push back. Gives you a verdict with specific scores.

Built from 100+ research sources — partner interviews, investment memos, deal data, portfolio analysis. Every question, red flag, and rejection pattern is based on how these firms actually operate.

27 skills across 5 tiers:
- Real VC firms (Sequoia, a16z, YC, Benchmark, Founders Fund, and 7 more)
- VC archetypes (Operator, Finance, Product, Network, Impact)
- Investment stages (Pre-seed through Growth)
- Regional (Silicon Valley, Latin America, Europe)
- Crypto (Paradigm, a16z Crypto)
- Benchmark Mode: run your pitch through all 27 perspectives at once

100% free. MIT licensed. Open source.

Hey PH! I'm Phelipe.

I built this because I was tired of practicing pitches with friends who say "sounds great!" when the idea has obvious holes.

Real VCs don't do that. They ask "Why now?" and won't let you dodge it. They calculate your burn multiple while you're still explaining the product. They pass on deals that 99% of people would fund.

I spent weeks researching how each firm actually operates — reading partner interviews, studying investment memos, analyzing their portfolios — and turned it all into simulation files that work with any AI assistant.

The YC skill will cut you off mid-sentence and ask how many users you have. The Benchmark skill won't invest unless all 5 partners agree. The Tiger Global skill will ask for your P&L before you finish your vision slide.

Try the Benchmark Mode if you want the full experience — it runs your pitch through 10 VC evaluation dimensions and gives you a readiness score out of 100. Most founders score 40-60. That's the point. You find the gaps before the real meeting.

Everything is open source and free. Would love contributions — especially skills for Asian, African, and Middle Eastern VCs.
